## Priority 
The purpose of a priority exercise is to help identify the no-brainer ideas from a backlog of great ideas. We will ask each of you to rank the the following ideas in two ways:

1. One hub before and during sessions (strongest theme — maps to empathy pain)
2. Capture encounters without admin guilt
3. Resource library and discovery (end “where did I put that?” tax)
4. Paths, journeys, and progress visibility
5. Link health and mentee engagement signals
6. Session scheduling and coaching support
7. Onboarding and coordinator handoff
8. Outcomes, continuity, and compliance
9. Compensation and recognition (confirm scope)
10. Magical / exploratory (YES AND parking lot)

### Observe
When making your observations provide two simple coma delimited lists. First order the ideas from lowest to highest impact, and then from least to most feasible. For example
```
impact: 3,2,1,9,4,8,10,5,6,7
feasibility: 3,1,2,5,6,8,4,7,9,10
```
Copy that list onto a markdown file in cursor, and then use opt-arrow hot keys to move lines up and down to get them in order, then list the numbers here in that order. 

### Reflect
Review the ranking observations below, then compile average rankings for each number, and group ideas under four categories: 
- High impact high feasibility ideas are considered **no-brainers**
- High impact low feasibility ideas are considered **big bets**
- Low impact High feasibility ideas are considered **utilities**
- Low impact and low feasibility ideas are **unwise**

## Needs
It needs exercise allows us to focus on what we're going to build to meet the needs of our users. In this case, we're going to focus on Marty the mentor, and the pages that she will use. 

### Observe
Observations are focused on Marti the Mentor persona and should be in the form
Marti needs a <page> where she can <enter/view/update> <data>
If you don't know what page it would be on, then just list is as a generic need. For example:
```
Marty needs a way to create a new Resource.
Marty needs a Resource page where she can view and update a resource. 
```
When you're ready to start, reply with a thumbs up, when you have emptied your mind of all observations, react with a check-mark. 

### Reflect
Work with the team to serialize the needs into a journey.
