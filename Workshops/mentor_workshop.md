# Coordinator Workshop - MentorHub Design Workshop

This workshop will help us to develop a better understanding of what we're creating for the mentor persona. As a reminder, we have identified that the mentor is responsible for finding Resources to share with a Craftsperson on their learning Journey. A Mentor records notes about an Encounter (meeting) with a Craftsperson. 

---

## Empathy Exercise
The purpose of this Empathy Mapping exercise is to focus our attention on Maria the Mentor and try to empathize with their current realities. 

Give the empathy exercise target a name and just enough context information to trigger a A sense of familiarity. Family circumstances, Hobbies, Interests, These are things that we can imagine quickly without biasing the empathy in any particular direction. 

### Observe
Please document your observations about Carol the Coordinator. Classify your observation it into one of these categories:

- **does** - What have you seen them do? What do you imagine they do?
- **says** - What have you heard them say?
- **thinks** - How do they reason about their work
- **feels** - What emotions do they show/share?

Example format:

```
does: Edits videos, Plays Disc Golf, drinks beer
says: Why are you doing that?
says: Let's go to Packs Pub
thinks: I'm the greatest editor in the world!
feels: Frustrated production takes so long
```

When you're ready to start, reply with a thumbs up, when you have emptied your mind of all observations, react with a check-mark.

### Reflect
Review the observations below and summarize them by category in the headings below that. Emphasize common observations. Highlight causal threads that allow you to follow a workflow.

#### Does

**Prepare for the encounter (consensus)**
- Checks mentee progress and curriculum before the next meeting
- Sorts learning links by topic and skill level; adjusts the path when a mentee is ahead or behind
- Hunts for the right resource—bookmarks, Google Docs, wikis, personal connections—often spending extra time before or after hours
- Wishes for **one place** to see links, paths, and past meetings (Mary); curriculum tracker for all mentees (Lucky)

**Run the encounter**
- Meets craftsperson on Discord voice; communicates to uplift, instruct, and listen
- Opens with focus questions: learning style, what to work on today, hardest part last time
- Recommends resources; provides brotherhood/sisterhood and meaningful, caring advice
- Takes notes during the session—Obsidian, notepad/BlackBerry, or Whisper record/transcribe—often quick and messy at first

**Follow through and coordinate**
- Works with coordinators when mentors are needed
- Structures notes later and sends links (“try this before we meet again”)
- Follows up on homework, interviews, and whether shared resources landed
- Commits to staying in touch between sessions
- Always scouting new resources and connections—actively curates and combines materials (“if I get two of these resources together, I might be able to do something”)

**Behind the scenes**
- Context-switches from own delivery work into deep focus on someone else’s growth
- Takes personal interest in each mentee; early dedication; feels accountable when the day does not go as planned

#### Says

**Session openers and coaching**
- “What would you like to focus on today?”
- “What are you interested in? How can I help you?”
- “Let’s take a look at your curriculum.”
- “What’s your learning style?”
- “What was the hardest part for you last time?”
- “You’re doing great, but let’s narrow your focus so you don’t get overwhelmed.”

**Resources and path**
- “Try this one link before we meet again.”
- “I will update your path to match where you are now.”
- “Let me look through my bookmarks—I have a really good guide on this somewhere.”

**Follow-up and honesty**
- “How did that interview go last week? Did that resource I sent make sense?”
- “I will keep in touch with you.”
- Meaningful advice delivered with a caring tone
- “I want to help, but I’m just underwater with my own project delivery deadlines this week.”
- “I wish there was more time.”

**Resource mindset**
- “I’m always looking for help and new resources.”

**Human moments**
- Venting about tooling friction (e.g., WSL)—mentor is a practitioner, not just a coach

#### Thinks

**Person-first, not tech-first (strong consensus)**
- Cares about the person, not just the technology
- Asks: How can I help in this person’s situation today? What do they need for their education?
- T-shaped engineering matters; **right order of learning beats more links**
- Success is the mentee’s breakthrough, not the mentor’s output

**Quality and fit of guidance**
- Hopes she is not overloading them; calibrates resources too advanced vs just right
- “Am I giving good advice, or just telling them how I did it?”
- How to deliver negative news without discouraging

**Admin and memory burden**
- Clear meeting notes save time every session—but messy notes create guilt if not cleaned up and sent
- If she does not follow up on notes, they might think she does not care about their progression
- Mentees are really benefiting from the time together when the relationship works

**Personal bar for impact (late observations)**
- If she helps even **one person** in a week, that counts as a good week
- Combines resources creatively—two good links together might unlock progress for a mentee
- Time is the limiting factor; wishes there were more hours for everyone who needs her

#### Feels

**Reward and pride (shared peak)**
- Proud of mentee accomplishments; deep fulfillment when they “get it” or hit a milestone
- Happy when a mentee asks a **real question from the work**
- Pride in knowing she is helping someone else

**Care and concern**
- Concerned about mentee wellbeing
- Relieved when meeting prep is quick
- Feels it is her job to **save the world one mentee at a time**—mission-driven, not transactional

**Pressure and accountability (late observations)**
- If the week was not successful, she has to **try harder** next time—not optional, personal
- Connects to taking the day personally when she cannot accomplish what she planned

**Friction, guilt, and overload (Lucky’s thread—widely echoed)**
- **“Where did I put that?” tax** — wasting time digging through browser history, Slack, email for one resource
- **Amnesia anxiety** — dread of not remembering where they left off or what homework was assigned
- **Administrative guilt** — fragmented notes not yet structured or emailed
- **Context-switching whiplash** — jumping from high-pressure own work into someone else’s career
- Tired when managing many mentees at once
- Imposter syndrome — worry about having the right answers or an effective style
- Takes the day personally when she cannot accomplish what she planned

**Causal thread (Does → Says → Thinks → Feels)**

```text
Prep (progress, links, path) → Discord session → notes / Whisper / Obsidian
        ↓                           ↓                    ↓
 "what interests you?"        person-first coaching    "I will keep in touch"
 "how can I help?"             hope not overloading      follow-up links & path updates
        ↓                           ↓                    ↓
 right order > more links      combine 2 resources       pride at breakthroughs;
 hunt + scout new resources    one person/week = win     mission: one mentee at a time
        ↓                           ↓                    ↓
 need 1 hub; wish more time    caring honest tone       guilt if notes lag;
                               "always looking for        try harder after bad week;
                                new resources"            amnesia + context-switch pain
```

## Big Ideas Exercise
The purpose of a big ideas exercise is to capture big ideas about how to improve the experience of a user. We want to focus on the meaningful outcome of an experience and not get bogged down in details at this point. The exercise is more interested in the quantity of observations than quality. We'd rather have your 10 weirdest ideas than your one best idea.

### Observe
Generate as many ideas as possible for improving the customer experience. Focus on meaningful outcomes rather than details. Include absurd or magical thinking!

Use this format for your ideas:

```
<who> does <what> and then <wow>
```

Example:

```
James clicks on the buy-it-now button, and then he can bypass the checkout process!
```

When you're ready to start, reply with a thumbs up, when you have emptied your mind of all observations, react with a check-mark.

### Reflect
Review all the ideas generated below, summarize them as a concise list and:
- Identify patterns and groupings
- Discuss and reconcile similar ideas
- Keep the floor open for "YES AND!" moments to build on others' ideas

#### 1. One hub before and during sessions (strongest theme — maps to empathy pain)
- **5 minutes before:** 3-bullet last-encounter summary *or* notes pop up on screen — *same moment, merge into one **Session Brief***
- One dashboard: every mentee, path, and upcoming meeting; daily focus when mentoring five people
- Start Discord encounter → system opens craftsperson profile tab for instant context
- Cross-device prep: phone vs laptop notes stay in sync

**Discuss:** Single **Mentor Command Center** vs separate “brief,” “dashboard,” and “encounter launcher.”

---

#### 2. Capture encounters without admin guilt
- Discord voice meeting auto-recorded in system—no manual note workflow
- Quick in-session note → auto-saves to **shared space** with craftsperson (no follow-up email)
- Notes saved and tied to mentee + session
- Messy bullets → clean summary for next time
- Messy unstructured thought → polished advice for mentee *(AI assist—pair with bullet cleanup)*

**Discuss:** One **Encounter Capture** flow: live notes + optional auto-record + AI cleanup, not three features.

---

#### 3. Resource library and discovery (end “where did I put that?” tax)
- Paste raw link → auto title, summary, read-time
- Online menu of educational links for quick reference (Luther)
- Search topic (e.g. “React basics”) → top resources used successfully by other mentors
- Reuse resources from other mentors—not sole source of content
- Onboarding wizard → first reusable **Resource Bundle** by topics she already teaches
- Two-minute voice clip attaches to a resource for all future mentees
- Photo of mentee project → suggest next step

**Discuss:** **Resource Hub** = personal library + org-wide search + smart metadata on paste.

---

#### 4. Paths, journeys, and progress visibility
- Create mentor learning path → one-click add to craftsperson journey
- Add link → system suggests where it fits on path
- New path → ready-made first-week plan to tweak
- New mentee mid-quarter → inherit starter path
- Mentee falls behind → path suggests shorter catch-up route
- Status board with checkboxes; accomplishments and proposed plan highly visible (Luther)
- Kat marks resource complete + rating → Maria sees progress and sentiment

**Discuss:** **Path Studio** = templates, auto-placement, catch-up routes, mentee-facing progress.

---

#### 5. Link health and mentee engagement signals
- Weekly email listing broken links in Maria’s resources
- Fix one link → every path using it updates automatically
- Check-in shows whether mentee **opened shared links**

**Discuss:** Same link registry powers broken-link alerts, bulk path updates, and open tracking.

---

#### 6. Session scheduling and coaching support
- Schedule encounter → mentee gets clear prep list before meeting
- Unsure before tough conversation → three gentle ways to open the meeting

**Discuss:** Lightweight **Session Kit**: prep list for mentee + conversation starters for mentor.

---

#### 7. Onboarding and coordinator handoff
- Maria completes onboarding → Carol has everything needed to match her with a craftsperson
- Sign up → first mentee match in under five minutes

**Discuss:** Mentor profile completeness vs speed-to-first-match—what’s the minimum viable onboarding?

---

#### 8. Outcomes, continuity, and compliance
- Month of encounters → auto-drafted **impact summary** for performance reviews
- End long mentorship → export **growth story** mentee keeps
- Pause platform → nothing deleted; resume exactly where she left off
- Leave platform → mentees retain paths and notes already shared
- GDPR removal button → data removed + compliance event recorded

**Discuss:** **Mentorship Archive** for export/retention vs **Right to Be Forgotten**—both needed.

---

#### 9. Compensation and recognition (confirm scope)
- Record encounters in system → paycheck (Mike)
- Tip jar: mentee tips → notification → “mentor goes shopping!” (Luther)

**Discuss:** Product feature vs org policy—is paid mentoring in MentorHub scope?

---

#### 10. Magical / exploratory (YES AND parking lot)
- Mentor + “mint tea” program → collaborated “best of” outcome (Daniel)
- Photo of resource → returns names, addresses, phone numbers (Daniel)
- Lose a detail → program finds it from other inputs (Daniel)
- At job site → lookup pushes search with targeted resources (Daniel)
- Struggling situation → program returns resource options (Daniel)

**Discuss:** Daniel’s thread = **context-aware resource finder** (situation/photo/keyword in → ranked resources out)—same engine as groups 3 and 4.

## Retrospective Exercise
The purpose of a retrospective exercise is to celebrate our successes and identify opportunities for continuous improvement. 

### Observe
Make observations grouped into these four categories:

- **question**: Any questions you might have regarding the scope or process
- **worked**: Things that worked well
- **change**: Things that need to change
- **ideas**: New ideas to try next time

Example format:
```
question: When will we do this again?
worked: Jill rocked as team lead
change: All of our deliverables were late
ideas: T-Shirt size tasks before the next sprint
```

When you're ready to start, reply with a thumbs up, when you have emptied your mind of all observations, react with a check-mark.

### Reflect
Review the observations and organize them as a markdown outline with these four category headings:

#### Questions
- Is the team starting to click with design workshops?
- How does everyone feel about the workshops?
- Will AI be permitted as much as we hope it will be?
- Is the application of our collaboration sufficient?

#### What Worked
- **Format and clarity:** Everyone using proper formatting; good preliminary instructions reduced confusion about what was being asked
- **Participation and ideas:** Strong team participation; several good ideas generated; whimsical ideas were taken seriously
- **Discussion quality:** Talking through unclear items helped contributors’ meaning land more accurately
- **Workshop rhythm:** Things went smoothly; instructions and expectations were easier to understand; team is loosening up and evolving
- **Repetition** of the exercise structure helped (Mary)
- **AI with guardrails:** AI was successfully used with stipulations (Daniel)
- **Mentor empathy input:** Reflect phase captured mentor observations, though some felt not fully targeted yet (Daniel)

#### What Needs to Change
- **Raw copy/paste from chat** — post distilled stickies, not full chat dumps → *ideas: shared format prompt; review chat output before posting*
- **Sticky-note brevity** — write only what fits on a 3×3 sticky; resist over-long posts (Mary)
- **Voice-to-text clarity** — transcription quality caused confusion → *idea: use audio instead when clarity is an issue*
- Daniel: no major process changes needed—**continue as is** for now

#### New Ideas to Try
- Create a **shared prompt** that describes the expected message format (Mike)
- **Always review chat/AI output** before posting to Discord (Mike)
- **Pre-workshop prompt** for upcoming retrospectives or big-ideas exercises to lay a concrete foundation (Daniel)
- Explore **collaborating on ideas beforehand**—acknowledged it may limit variety of fresh ideas (Daniel)
- Prefer **audio over voice-to-text** when clarity matters (Mary)
