# Three Ways to Fund the Future of Tech Talent

## Introduction
At the Agile Learning Institute (ALI), we turn potential into profession through our Stage0 Launch framework: *“A rough idea + 4 apprentices + mentoring + Stage0 tools”* → a live cloud service ready to onboard users.

Our flagship project, **MentorHub**, is a SaaS platform where mentorship powers lifelong learning for software engineers. It is built entirely by interns who gain real‑world experience while delivering a product that will serve thousands.

To sustain and expand this impact, we are launching three targeted fundraising campaigns. Your support will directly fund paid internships, equip our apprentices with modern tools, and keep our operations running as we scale.

## 2️⃣ Equip Interns with Modern Developer Workstations
**The Need**  
Our interns currently rely on a mix of personal laptops and aging workstations. To develop efficiently with containerized microservices, Vue 3, and CI/CD pipelines, they need reliable, modern hardware.

**Impact of Your Gift**  
- **$1,200** per workstation provides a laptop/desktop with ≥16 GB RAM, SSD storage, and a multi‑core CPU—enough to run Docker, Kubernetes‑kind, and IDEs smoothly.  
- We aim to equip all four interns (Daniel, Mary, Luther, Lucky) with identical machines to eliminate environment discrepancies and streamline pair‑programming.  
- Faster build/test cycles mean more time spent on feature work and less on troubleshooting.  

**Quote from Mary (Data Lead)**  
> “When my queries run instantly and my Vue components hot‑reload without lag, I can focus on designing the MongoDB configurator schema instead of fighting the toolchain.”
